Internal Architecture Notes
===========================

Service topology
----------------
The platform is split into three services: the edge gateway, the payments
service, and the ledger. The edge gateway terminates TLS, authenticates
requests, and applies rate limiting before forwarding traffic to the payments
service. The payments service owns all business logic for charges, refunds, and
payouts. The ledger is an append-only store of double-entry accounting records;
it is the system of record for balances.

Data storage
------------
The payments service uses PostgreSQL as its primary database. Hot read traffic
is served from a Redis cache with a 30-second TTL. The ledger is stored in a
separate PostgreSQL instance and is never mutated in place — corrections are
posted as compensating entries.

Consistency model
------------------
Charges are written to PostgreSQL first and then asynchronously projected into
the ledger via an outbox pattern. Because projection is asynchronous, the ledger
is eventually consistent with the payments database, typically within two
seconds. Reconciliation jobs run hourly to detect and alert on any divergence.

Deployment
----------
Services are deployed as containers orchestrated by Kubernetes. Each service
runs a minimum of three replicas across two availability zones. Database
migrations are applied by a dedicated job that runs before the new application
version is rolled out.
